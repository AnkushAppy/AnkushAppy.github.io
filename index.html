Risks I take

Learning only happens at the edge of what you already know. These are the bets I'm comfortable making.

01

Prototype before the playbook exists

I try frameworks and ideas while they're still rough — DSPy for autonomous workflows, new agent patterns, experimental eval pipelines. I'd rather break something in a notebook than ship blind faith to production.

02

Replace what already works

Migrating legacy systems — Neo4j knowledge graphs to embedding-based search, old NLP pipelines to LLM orchestration. The risk is downtime and regression; the reward is a system you actually understand.

03

Benchmark, then decide

I don't pick tools by hype. I run latency sweeps, retrieval@k evals, and load tests — then commit. The risk is time spent measuring; the alternative is guessing at scale.

04

Explain it visually or I don't get it

Every topic on this page has a canvas — animated pipelines, isometric graphs, before/after charts. If I can't draw the flow, I probably don't understand it yet.
